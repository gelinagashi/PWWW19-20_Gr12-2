With the naked eye, the stars may all appear to be similar,
and nothing more than pinholes in the sky, but the reality is that
the stars come in a wide, almost stupefying range of sizes, masses,
colors, compositions, and spectra.

There are 88 modern constellations occupying different
regions of the sky, with the 12 zodiac constellations
situated within a 9° band either side of the ecliptic
plane. This 18 ° wide imaginary line traces the apparent
path that the Sun, Moon, and planets take over the course of a year.
